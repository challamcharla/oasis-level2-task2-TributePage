A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

APJ Abdul Kalam, the 11th President of India, was a renowned scientist and visionary leader. He played a crucial role in the development of India's missile and space research programs. Known as the "Missile Man of India," Kalam inspired generations with his humility and dedication. His books, like "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds," continue to inspire and motivate people worldwide. Kalam's legacy as a teacher, scientist, and President remains an enduring source of inspiration for aspiring minds. l

Early Life

☛ Birth and Family: Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, a small town in the state of Tamil Nadu, India. He was born into a modest Tamil Muslim family.
☛ Educational Background: Kalam's early education took place in Rameswaram and Ramanathapuram. He was an exceptional student with a keen interest in science and mathematics. Despite facing financial constraints, he worked hard and earned a degree in aeronautical engineering from Madras Institute of Technology (MIT) in 1958.
☛ Career in Aerospace: After completing his studies, Kalam joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) as a scientist. He later moved to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and contributed significantly to the development of India's space and missile programs.
☛ Contribution to SLV Project: In the early 1980s, Kalam served as the Project Director for India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle program (SLV-III). The successful launch of SLV-III in 1980 marked a significant milestone in India's space endeavors.
☛ Leadership in DRDO: Kalam's contributions to India's defense capabilities were also notable. He served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister of India and the Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) in the 1990s, overseeing several critical defense projects.

Scientist Career

☛ APJ Abdul Kalam's scientist career was marked by groundbreaking contributions in India's space and missile programs.
☛ He played a pivotal role in the successful development and testing of India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III).
☛ As the Chief Scientific Adviser and Secretary of DRDO, he contributed significantly to India's defense research and development.
☛ His expertise in aerospace and missile technology earned him the title "Missile Man of India.
☛ " Kalam's scientific career laid the foundation for India's advancements in space exploration and defense capabilities.

Personal Life

☛ In his personal life, APJ Abdul Kalam was known for his simplicity and humility.
☛ He remained unmarried throughout his life and dedicated himself to his work and the welfare of the nation.
☛ Kalam had a strong spiritual inclination and was deeply influenced by the teachings of Swami Vivekananda.
☛ He was an avid reader and enjoyed writing poetry in his leisure time.
☛Despite his fame and success, Kalam maintained a frugal lifestyle and continued to be deeply connected with the youth, often encouraging them to pursue their dreams.

Last Days

☛ APJ Abdul Kalam remained actively engaged in various pursuits, despite his age. On July 27, 2015, he was delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management (IIM) Shillong when he suddenly collapsed.
☛ He was immediately taken to the Bethany Hospital, where efforts were made to revive him, but unfortunately, he passed away due to cardiac arrest at the age of 83.
☛ Kalam's sudden demise was met with a wave of shock and grief across India and the world. He was known as the "People's President" and had won the hearts of millions with his inspiring leadership and dedication to the nation.
☛In his last moments, he was doing what he loved most - interacting with students and sharing his wisdom.
☛ His legacy continues to live on, and he remains a cherished figure, especially among the youth, who continue to draw inspiration from his life and achievements.

Awards & Rewards

☛ Bharat Ratna: In 1997, Kalam was bestowed with India's highest civilian award, the Bharat Ratna, for his exceptional contributions to science and technology, and for his role as a visionary leader.
☛ Padma Bhushan: He received the Padma Bhushan in 1981 for his distinguished service in defense research and development.
☛ Padma Vibhushan: In 1990, Kalam was honored with the Padma Vibhushan, the second-highest civilian award in India, in recognition of his contributions to the country's space and missile programs.
☛ Von Braun Award: In 2013, he received the prestigious Von Braun Award from the National Space Society in the United States for his significant contributions to the aerospace field.
☛ Hoover Medal: In 2009, Kalam became the first Asian to receive the Hoover Medal from ASME (American Society of Mechanical Engineers) Foundation, recognizing his outstanding public service contributions.
☛ Doctorates and Honorary Degrees: Kalam was bestowed with numerous honorary doctorates and degrees from universities and institutions worldwide, recognizing his immense contributions to science, technology, and education.
